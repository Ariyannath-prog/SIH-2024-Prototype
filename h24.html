Botanical Name: Bacopa monnieri

Common name: Brahmi

Bacopa monnieri, commonly known as Brahmi, thrives in tropical and subtropical regions. It grows in wetlands, along riverbanks, and in marshy areas. Preferring warm, humid climates, it requires abundant moisture and can be found in shallow, waterlogged soil. Bacopa monnieri is cultivated by sowing seeds in well-drained, wet soil or hydroponic setups. It thrives in warm, sunny conditions, with consistent moisture. Transplant seedlings into pots or garden beds when they are a few inches tall. Bacopa monnieri, also known as Brahmi, is used in traditional medicine for its cognitive-enhancing effects. It is believed to improve memory, reduce anxiety, and support overall mental health. Additionally, it has antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, potentially aiding in the treatment of neurological disorders and promoting general well-being.
